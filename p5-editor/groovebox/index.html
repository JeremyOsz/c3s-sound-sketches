Mini groovebox (4 tracks)

A tiny 16-step sequencer driving four synth voices: kick, noise perc, subtractive bass, and a simple FM lead. It’s intentionally small and “toy” — but built from the same building blocks as the other sketches.

Controls guide (what each control does)

Tip: push Master filter (Low-pass ← 0 → High-pass) left or right, and crank Master filter resonance (Q) for classic “DJ filter” motion.

Play / Stop
Starts/stops the sequencer clock and audio scheduling.
Tap tempo
Tap quarter notes; the groovebox sets BPM from the time between taps.
BPM
Tempo for the 16-step sequencer (16th notes).
Swing
Delays off-beat 16ths (odd steps) by a percentage of the step length.
Volume
Master output volume (post-filters).
Step grid
Click steps to toggle them on/off. The gold outline shows the current playhead step.
Mute (checkbox per track)
Silences that track (still shows playhead).
Solo (button per track)
If any track is soloed, only soloed tracks are heard (mute still applies).
Track levels
Per-track level scaling applied when scheduling each hit/note (a simple “velocity” control).
Master filter (Low-pass ← 0 → High-pass), Master filter resonance (Q)
A bipolar master filter over the whole groovebox mix: left is low-pass, right is high-pass, and the center position bypasses the filter.
Melodic low-pass filter, Low-pass frequency (melodic), Low-pass resonance (Q, melodic)
A low-pass filter control applied to the melodic tracks (bass + lead). Toggle it off to bypass.
Lead S&H pitch, Lead AM
Recap controls on the lead: sample-and-hold pitch variation chooses a held semitone offset every few steps, and AM adds tremolo-style amplitude movement.
